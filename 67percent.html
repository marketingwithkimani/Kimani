The Oversight

Every Industry Believes the Same Wrong Thing

That the real customers are the ones ready to buy today. The people searching for your product, comparing options, requesting quotes. So companies design their entire marketing systems around them, ads, sales funnels, landing pages, everything focused on the small percentage that is ready now.

But here is what the numbers quietly show across almost every market in Africa and beyond: that group is tiny. Most people are not ready today. Not next week. Sometimes not even this year. Yet those same people will eventually become customers — and they will do so with far more conviction and loyalty than anyone who was rushed into a decision by a discount code and an urgency timer.

The companies scrambling over immediate conversions are fighting over scraps. They are competing for the loudest voice in a shrinking room, while the real market, the majority, watches from outside the door, deciding who deserves to be let in.

The Insight

The Invisible Majority is Already In Your Market

They are not absent. They are present — quietly forming opinions about your brand based on how you show up, every single day.

Think about how people actually make decisions. A family considering changing banks. A company evaluating a new telecom provider. A traveler deciding which airline they trust. These decisions rarely happen instantly. They happen slowly — over months, sometimes years. People watch. They listen. They notice how you behave. They observe how others speak about you. They pay attention to whether you are present when they need you and absent when they do not.

In African markets, this pattern is particularly pronounced. The social fabric of decision-making here is dense and relational. Before a business signs a major contract, the CEO has almost always already spoken to someone they trust about you. Before a consumer makes a big purchase, they have asked around. The 67% market is not irrational. It is deeply, intelligently social.

And when that moment finally arrives — when they are ready to decide — they choose the company that has quietly earned their confidence. Not the one with the loudest ad. Not the one with the biggest promise. The one that has been there. The one that has been consistent. The one that has, over time, become the safe, obvious choice.

"Companies that chase immediate sales are fighting over scraps. Companies that understand the 67% are building something far more powerful: future demand, earned in the present."

The Gap

Why Focusing Only on Today's Buyers Destroys Tomorrow's Growth

When a company builds its entire marketing machine around the 33% who are ready now, it creates a deeply fragile business model. It must constantly hunt for new leads because the pipeline never builds depth. It must spend more with every campaign because it has no trust equity stored in the market. Every quarter becomes a new battle. Every sales target feels impossible because it was never designed to compound.

The alternative is not slower growth. It is smarter growth. Companies that invest in the 67% are building a reservoir. Every piece of honest communication, every consistent touchpoint, every moment of genuine value delivered without expectation, it all accumulates into market trust that eventually converts at scale, at lower cost, and with higher lifetime value.

The Shift

The Companies That Win Ask a Different Question

🧠

They Ask "How Do We Stay Present?"

Not "how do we sell today?" but "how do we remain relevant and trustworthy in our market until the moment arrives?" That question changes everything about how they communicate.

🌱

They Build Trust as an Asset

Trust is not a feeling — it is a business asset with measurable value. The most trusted brand in a market commands better prices, higher loyalty, and unprompted referrals.

🏆

They Play the Long Game

When the 67% finally decide, they have already made their choice in their minds. The winning company got there first — not with more ads, but with more presence and more genuine value.

"The decision is already made before the conversation starts. The 67% market chooses in silence, long before they raise their hand. Your job is not to convince — it is to have already earned their trust."

The Transformation

What Your Business Looks Like When You Win the 67%

Your pipeline no longer runs dry at the end of every quarter. Because the trust you have been building for the past eighteen months is now converting — quietly, steadily, powerfully. New clients come to you already convinced. Your sales team closes faster because the market has already done the emotional work. Your marketing spend becomes more efficient because you are not starting from zero with every campaign.

More than that: your brand becomes the reference point in your market. When someone asks for a recommendation in your category, your name comes first. Not because you paid for it. Because you earned it. That is the compounding power of building for the 67%.
