Wasn't Built For Your Market.

Here, someone can think about buying land for 6 months. Talk to friends. Ask family. Drive past the same plot three times. Save slowly. Doubt. Come back again. And only then — decide. But the marketing you've been using behaves like the decision happens in a single moment.

📺

You Saw a Bigger Screen.

Think about the last time you bought a TV. You weren't shopping. You were at a friend's place. Saw a bigger screen. Said "mine is getting old." That was the beginning — an unconscious thought that started a journey.

👁️

Then You Started Noticing.

Suddenly TVs were everywhere. Scrolling at night — one appears. Watching YouTube — another shows. Passing a shop — you slow down. You hadn't decided to buy. You were in the 67%. Thinking. Watching. Moving closer.

Clean. Sharp. Well-designed. "Best price. Limited stock." You pause. You imagine it in your sitting room. You even calculate if you can afford it... then you scroll. Not because you didn't want it. But because you weren't ready.

🏆

You Bought From the One That Stayed.

Weeks later, you bought. But not from that ad. Not from the one that rushed you. You bought from the brand that felt familiar. The one that somehow understood your pace. That's the power of the 67% strategy.

The Decision Gap

How Buyers Actually Move

There is a gap between when someone becomes aware and when they decide.
This gap is where businesses are chosen or forgotten.

🌱 Trigger Moment

A casual observation creates an unconscious desire. The journey begins without the person even knowing it.

🔍 Silent Research

They start noticing. Googling quietly. Comparing without committing. This phase can last weeks or months.

⏳ The Long Middle

← This is the 67% zone. Most businesses go silent here. Kimani's system stays present, building trust without pressure.

📣 Your Competitor's Ad

"Buy now." "Last chance." Interrupts the journey but doesn't move the buyer. They scroll past. Nothing built. Nothing remembered.

💡 Readiness Moment

Something shifts — a pay rise, a life event, a final nudge. They are now ready. They reach for the brand that has been with them all along.

✅ The Decision

They don't compare. They don't search again. They go back to the one that felt familiar. The one that understood their pace. That should be you.

The Hard Question

What Are You Saying To Them
Right Now?

They scroll past your post. They like your ad. Then they disappear. You assume they weren't serious. But what if they're exactly where you were with that TV? Thinking. Moving closer. Waiting to feel ready.

When they are just starting to think —

What do they see from you? Is there content that speaks to someone at the beginning of their journey, or only to someone who has already decided?

When they are considering —

What do you give them? Something that answers the unspoken questions? Or just another "buy now" that they'll scroll past?

When they're not ready yet but will be —

How do you stay in their mind? What makes them think of you first when the moment finally arrives — and not your competitor?
